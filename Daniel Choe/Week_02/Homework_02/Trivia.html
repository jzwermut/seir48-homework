Grace Brewster Murray Hopper (December 9, 1906 - January 1, 1992)

Interesting Facts:

  • She is credited with coining the terms "bug" and "de-bug" as related to computer errors.
  • While working on the UNIVAC I and II, Hopper pioneered the idea of automatic programming and explored new ways to use the computer to code. In 1952 she developed the first compiler called A-0, which translated mathematical code into machine-readable codeâ€”an important step toward creating modern programming languages.

What Joel may not know about Grace:

  • She was the oldest serving officer in the U.S. Armed Forces (retired at age 79).
  • In 1991 she was the first woman who was awarded by President George Bush the National Medal of Technology (the nation's highest technology award).
  • Possessed the 'knack'. At the age of seven she took apart her alarm clock to find out how it worked. Unable to reassemble it, she took it to pieces the other seven clocks she found in the house before her mother discovered what was happening.
